The Style Workshop is a boutique residential interior design studio on Sydney's Lower North Shore, with extensive experience across Neutral Bay's diverse range of property types.

Neutral Bay sits at the heart of the Lower North Shore and reflects it: a suburb where terrace homes, pre-war apartments, contemporary renovations and new builds share the same streets. It is a practical, well-connected suburb with a strong community character, and its interior design challenges are among the most varied TSW encounters regularly.

Apartment work is a particular strength here. Neutral Bay has a high density of apartment stock, from original 1930s and 1950s buildings with their particular dimensions and quirks, to more recent developments that often need considered layering to feel finished rather than furnished. Getting spatial planning right in a compact footprint is a discipline of its own, and one that Emily Webster, principal designer at TSW, has developed across many projects in the area.

House clients in Neutral Bay tend to be families undertaking renovations, often working with builders and wanting a design direction that is both practical for daily life and resolved enough to hold up at the level of finish they are investing in. This intersection of the functional and the considered is where TSW works best.

Apartments, terraces and family homes

Every property type in Neutral Bay calls for a slightly different approach. Apartments reward careful space planning, material restraint and the kind of furniture selection that does not fight the footprint. Period buildings benefit from decisions that acknowledge the original architecture without being enslaved to it. Family homes need interiors robust enough for real life and considered enough to feel worth coming home to.

Across all of these, the TSW process is consistent: discover, concept, refine, deliver. What changes is the expression. The aim is always the same, a home that feels settled, individual and quietly excellent.
